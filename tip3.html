Tip #3: Try me!

No, really, try the foods! (And try hovering over the pictures) Although I said I would be giving your tips for good meals to have at home, it's good to expand your palette so that you can add different flavors and dishes to your meal repertoire. These may be fancier, but they can introduce you to new things.


Scallops may seem scary, but when cooked correctly, they are a great mild seafood that's usually served in a rich clarified butter.
I really enjoy lobster bisque. Again, I know seafood can be scary, but this creamy, buttery bisque makes for an excellent pairing.
Before I got food poisoning from shrimp, I really liked them. And I never got food poisoning from the shrimp I made at home. And I loved lemon-butter-garlic shrimp, specifically, like you see in this picture.
Fresh seafood from the coast is the best! This is Pike Place Market in Seattle, WA.
And sometimes, you may find out you don't like the food. In the case of pho, for me, I don't like lemongrass.
