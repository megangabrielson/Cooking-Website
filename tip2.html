Tip #2: FODMAP for funsies?

So, one of my friends is on a FODMAP diet.
FODMAP: fermentable oligo-, di-, mono-saccharides, and polyols
Here's what she had to say about it: DON'T. (Well... unless you have to for health reasons). Let's take a look at some of the restricted foods on this diet.


According to Harvard, you shouldn't eat the
following for a low FODMAP diet:
  • Lactose
  • Fructose
  • Fructans
  • GOS
  • Polyols
What are these?
  • Lactose: like normal milk-y stuff
  • Fructose: like...
    • Natural sugars
    • Sweeteners
    • Anything with high-fructose corn syrup.
      (Which we figured out, is about ... everything)
  • Fructans:
    • Gluten
    • Lots of different veggies
  • GOS:
    • Different kinds of beans... like any beans
    • More veggies!
  • Polyols
    • A lot of fruits
    • More veggies, really?
    • Things for your throat or breath

So what does this diet include?

No gum... no apples... no ice cream... :( What's even left? Meat. Meat is left. And lettuce, and unnatural milks (how does one milk an almond? I don't know. But it is pretty good!), and bananas, and quinoa and carrots. Oh, and some other stuff, but you get the point.

So FODMAP for funsies? I think not. I'll pass.

So besides a rant about FODMAP, what's my cooking tip? Well, if you aren't on a diet, it will make it a lot easier to cook for yourself. And a lot cheaper. But, sometimes you can't help it.
